Spring '16 Rush

When designing marketing materials, I loosely follow these steps: Brainstorm, Research, Plan, Create, Layout, Impact, Feedback.

Brainstorm

During my brainstorm, I figure out the purpose of the flyer, the target audience, and what message I am hoping to convey. For this flyer, I settled on a Lego theme because it is a part of most engineersâ€™ childhood and can thus establish an instant emotional connection. Establishing this emotional connection was my number one goal in order to increase the chances of a busy college student actually reading the flyer.

Research

To flesh out my idea, I research good examples of club flyers and how other designers have used Legos in their promotional materials.

Plan

Here, I come up with what information I need to include in the flyer.

  • Rush Spring 2015
  • "Get Built" slogan
  • Theta Tau Professional Engineering Fraternity
  • Lego man

Create

Then, I create the vectors and choose a font.

Layout

Now that I have all of the components, I think of the hierarchy of visual elements and how I will bring a focus to the most important elements.

Impact

This step is the most time-consuming: adding impact to the design. Usually, I try to increase contrast to make the flyer more eye-catching and add depth through shadows and colors.

Feedback

I receive feedback from my designer friends, the people I am working with, and the target audience to see if the design is effective.

Rush Apparel

Finally, for the rush apparel, I went with a simple classic black longsleeve look.

Fall '16 Rush

As the Fall 2016 Recruitment Chair, my co-chair and I decided on a theme centering around Big Hero 6, a Disney movie centered around a robot friend (technology). The main targeted audience of this rush were female engineering students in order to help improve the diversity of Theta Tau. Since Baymax is such a lovable Disney character, I thought that Big Hero 6 would be perfect to help establish an instant emotional connection.
