A website that provides a database of all redistricting data for California.

My Role

Redefine the user experience for data searchability
Implement the new UI/UX using HTML/CSS/JS/PHP

The Challenge

Due to the vast and varying categories of redistricting data, the organization of the site is key to helping people find the data they need. As the sole developer for Statewide Database, I had a great degree of freedom to implement my creative vision. Correspondingly, I designed a custom homepage, navigation bar, and footer to differentiate the site from others. From there, I utilized JavaScript and PHP ton obtain the needed functionality.

The Process - Navigation

My main focus for navigation was on those users who did not know the specific files they were looking for. I utilized the header navigation bar as well as the footer to provide two avenues for navigating the site. Within the nested mega navigation bar, I organized the data by years, and then further subcategorized it by type of data. The footer I designed took a different approach, instead displaying the most popular categories subdivided by years of data available.

The Process - Search

For users who DO know exactly what file they are looking for, I developed a categorical search engine connected to a MySQL database. I populated the database by crawling through the archives and parsing the file names to categorize them.

Reflections

The trickiest aspect of this project was the amount of research involved. It was an immense effort to architect the best way to intuitively categorize redistricting data. On top of this, implementing the multi-level mega navigation bar was also a challenge. Although the final website has not been deployed to the official site yet, I thoroughly enjoyed the project.
