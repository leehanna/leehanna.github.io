My Role

March 2015 - Feb 2016
As the sole web developer for Statewide Database (SWDB), I worked on the UI/UX Design, frontend, and backend using HTML, CSS, JS, PHP, and Bootstrap. My main goal was to improve the navigability of the website and modernize the UI.

Navigation UX

Problem

How do you help people find the information they need?

Research

  • First, I poured over books around the office to gain a basic understanding of redistricting data.
  • The current organization of the website is very disorganized and unintuitive. The user has to click through several different links to get to a page with all the files, which are placed in tables.
  • Then, I visited redistricting websites to see how they were organized. Although none of the websites really offered a simple way to search through the categories, I was able to gain a better idea of what works and what doesn’t.

Hypothesis

  • Offer two different ways of categorization
  • A versatile navigation bar that is well-organized and intuitive
  • Navigation should also be possible within the actual data page

Header Navigation Bar

  • I organized the different categories into a mega navigation bar similar to Amazon’s that has multiple nested drop downs. To nest them in an organized fashion, I made a side panel that displays more options on mouse hover.
  • Similar to Amazon’s mega navigation bar, I opted to display additional panels on mouse hover instead of on click to make using the navigation bar more fluid and less of a commitment.
  • The main navigation bar has the main categories of redistricting data, election data, and additional resources. The next level of organization was by years, and I showed all of the related data of that year in one place.

Mega Footer

  • Additionally, I offered a different organization structure in the footer, where I grouped all the data by type and showed all the different years of that data.
  • The office frequently receives inquiries regarding location certain files, so I placed a contact form right in the footer so that it is accessible on every page.
Search

For users who DO know exactly what file they are looking for, I developed a categorical search engine connected to a MySQL database. I populated the database by crawling through the archives and parsing the file names to categorize them.

Reflections

The trickiest aspect of this project was the amount of research involved. It was an immense effort to architect the best way to intuitively categorize redistricting data. On top of this, implementing the multi-level mega navigation bar was also a challenge. Although the final website has not been deployed to the official site yet, I thoroughly enjoyed the project.
