How I got into design

When I was in high school in 2011, I interned as a layout designer at a publishing company, which started my design career. I was amazed at how much thought went behind every single element on a page, and how these elements can subconsciously influence the viewer. For a brief time, I wanted to go into business since I loved using design thinking to come up with marketing strategies.

In the end, computer science won me over when I first tasted that magical feeling from solving what seems like a puzzle to build something you can interact with. Even while studying computer science in college, I was never far from design or marketing strategy since I somehow ended up designing publicity materials for all six of the clubs I was involved in.

Now, I am aspiring to become a product designer since it luckily combines all of the fields I was ever intrigued by into one: psychology, business, tech, and design.
