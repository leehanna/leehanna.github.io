2. Experience Strategy

Card Sorting

The key difference in the searchability of the apps I examined was how they organized their grocery items. To understand users’ conceptual models of grocery categories, I conducted a card sorting exercise with 3 young tech savvy business professionals and roughly grouped the results to spot similarities.

Information Architecture

After analyzing the results from the card sorting exercise and what kind of category groups competitors chose to use, I began to structure the content in a sitemap.
While arranging the items in the sitemap, I thought about how each page is connected with other pages. For example, in grocery stores, items on sale are prominently highlighted with special price tags in an aisle with other similar products to catch shoppers’ attention. I modeled this in my sitemap by having a section within the Department section that displays Coupon deals (represented by the yellow arrows).

Wireframes

After structuring the content of the app, I arranged the information on each page with wireframes. I used SketchApp with the Teracy wireframe kit to create them more quickly.

Wireframe User Testing

Once I made the wireframes, I created a low-fidelity, interactive prototype to see how they performed with real users. Users were asked to accomplish 3 tasks: 1) signup, 2) find a product, 3) add it to the shopping cart, 4) checkout. I then refined my wireframes with the resulting findings.

Reflections

As with any ecommerce website, there were a lot of user interactions to think about when designing this application, so this project was very fun. I narrowed my scope to just the mobile app because of the variety of these possible interactions. However, next time I have a more complex project, I would like to tackle designing responsively from the beginning.
